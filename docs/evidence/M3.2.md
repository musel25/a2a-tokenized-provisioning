## Surprises / deviations from the plan

- **The real router reviewed my payloads.** Three live-fire corrections nothing else
  would have caught: (1) RFC 7951 encodes a YANG `empty` leaf as `[null]`, not `{}`
  (`violate-action drop`); (2) Get responses namespace keys that come from YANG
  *augments* (`srl_nokia-acl-policers:policer-templates`) — plain key lookups silently
  miss, hence `_denamespace`; (3) a template delete is refused while its attachment
  exists *even inside one Set transaction* — deletes are ordered attachment-first.
- **SR Linux rate-limits gNMI CONNECTIONS (60/min)** — a dial-per-operation adapter
  locked itself out of its own router mid-suite. Two fixes: the provisioner now caches
  one connection per device (the realistic adapter shape anyway), and the lab raises
  the limit at boot (`srl1-init.cli`).
- iperf3's UDP client JSON reports the SENDER rate; the received (post-policer) rate is
  `sent × (1 − lost_percent/100)`. The first version of the acceptance test happily
  measured 100 Mbps *through* the policer because of this.
- FakeNet was promoted to `netctl.mock.MockProvisioner` per plan; e2e keeps the stage
  name as an alias (one-line change, zero test edits).
